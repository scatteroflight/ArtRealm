Oscar-Claude Monet

(1840-1926) Watch Lifetime -

"What keeps my heart awake is colourful silence."


  • Birth

    1840

    Born in Paris, France later moved to Normandy in 1845. Monet was a famous French painter, who was one of the founders of French Impressionist painting and the most consistent and prolific practitioner of the movement's philosophy of expressing one's perceptions before nature, especially as applied to plein air landscape painting.

  • The Woman in the Green Dress

    1866

    Monet displays the Woman in the Green Dress in the salon. Some mistake this for being a Edouard Manet painting, causing Manet to dislike Monet. He marries his first wife Camille in 1870.

  • Woman with a Parasol

    1875

    Monet paints this after his first exhibit the preceeding year. His wife Camille dies in 1879.

  • Rouen Cathedral Series

    1892

    In winter Monet begins painting the Rouan Cathedral series depiecting the Cathedral in varying levels of light

  • Water Lilies Series

    1896

    Monet begins painting the Water Lilies series, a series of approximately 250 oil paintings.The paintings depict his flower garden at his home in Giverny, and were the main focus of his artistic production during the last thirty years of his life. Many of the works were painted while Monet suffered from cataracts.

  • Japanese Bridge and Water Lily Pond

    1899

    The Water Lilies series begins to take shape as he paints one of his most widely recognized paintings.

  • Houses of Parliament Series

    1900

    In February 1900 Monet starts the Houses of Parliament series. Similar to the Cathedral, it depicts the buildings in different lights.

  • Nenúfares

    1904

    Monet starts interpreting and capturing his beloved water lilies in different patterns of brushstrokes.

  • View of San Giorgio Maggiore

    1908

    He travels to Venice with his second wife Alice and paints several scenes of Venice. Alice dies n 1911.

  • Large Watter Lillies

    1916

    Monet painted the Large Water Lillies including the panorama painings and triptychs.

  • Death

    1926

    Monet dies of lung cancer at the age of 86, and is buried in Giverny. At his funeral Georges Clemenceau removed the black cloth draped over the coffin, and replaced it with a flower pattern cloth.
